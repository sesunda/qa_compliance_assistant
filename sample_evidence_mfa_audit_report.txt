# Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Audit Report

**Audit Period:** October 1 - October 31, 2025  
**Report Date:** November 5, 2025  
**Auditor:** Internal Audit Department  
**Report ID:** AUD-MFA-2025-Q4  

---

## Executive Summary

This audit assessed the organization's Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) implementation across all critical systems. Overall, MFA controls are operating effectively with a 98% adoption rate.

**Overall Rating:** ✅ **SATISFACTORY**

---

## Audit Scope

Systems reviewed:
- Microsoft 365 (Email, SharePoint, Teams)
- Azure Cloud Infrastructure
- VPN Remote Access
- Administrative Accounts
- Financial Systems (SAP, QuickBooks)
- HR Systems (Workday)

---

## Findings

### ✅ Strengths

1. **High Adoption Rate**
   - 98% of users have MFA enabled (Target: 95%)
   - 100% of administrative accounts use MFA
   - 100% of VPN access protected by MFA

2. **Strong Authentication Methods**
   - 85% use authenticator apps (Microsoft/Google Authenticator)
   - 10% use hardware security keys (YubiKey)
   - 5% use SMS (approved for low-risk only)

3. **Effective Monitoring**
   - Real-time MFA failure alerts configured
   - Monthly compliance reporting in place
   - Automated enforcement for new accounts

4. **User Training**
   - 95% completion rate for MFA training
   - Help desk tickets for MFA reduced by 60% YoY

### ⚠️ Areas for Improvement

1. **SMS Usage** (Low Priority)
   - 5% of users still using SMS codes
   - Recommendation: Migrate to authenticator apps by Q1 2026

2. **Backup Codes** (Medium Priority)
   - Only 40% of users have stored backup recovery codes
   - Recommendation: Enforce backup code download during MFA setup

3. **Third-Party Contractors** (Medium Priority)
   - 12 contractor accounts without MFA (2% of total)
   - Recommendation: Implement MFA requirement in contracts
