# Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Policy

**Document Type:** Information Security Policy  
**Policy Number:** ISP-MFA-001  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025  
**Version:** 1.0  

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes requirements for Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) to protect organizational systems and data from unauthorized access.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all users, employees, contractors, and third parties accessing company information systems.

## 3. Policy Statement

### 3.1 MFA Requirements

- All users MUST enable MFA for accessing:
  - Email systems (Office 365, Gmail, etc.)
  - Cloud infrastructure (Azure, AWS, GCP)
  - Remote access systems (VPN, Remote Desktop)
  - Administrative accounts and privileged access
  - Financial and HR systems

### 3.2 Acceptable Authentication Factors

**Knowledge Factors (Something you know):**
- Passwords/Passphrases

**Possession Factors (Something you have):**
- Mobile authenticator apps (Microsoft Authenticator, Google Authenticator, Authy)
- Hardware security keys (YubiKey, Titan Security Key)
- SMS codes (acceptable for low-risk systems only)

**Inherence Factors (Something you are):**
- Biometric authentication (fingerprint, facial recognition)

### 3.3 Implementation Standards

1. **Primary Factor:** Complex password (minimum 12 characters)
2. **Secondary Factor:** Authenticator app or hardware key (PREFERRED) or SMS (ACCEPTABLE)
3. **High-Risk Systems:** Require authenticator app or hardware key only

### 3.4 Exemptions

- Systems with technical limitations may be exempted with CISO approval
- All exemptions must be documented and reviewed quarterly

## 4. Responsibilities

### 4.1 Users
- Enable and maintain MFA on all applicable accounts
- Safeguard authentication devices
- Report lost or compromised authentication factors immediately

### 4.2 IT Department
- Implement and maintain MFA infrastructure
- Provide user training and support
- Monitor MFA adoption and compliance

### 4.3 Security Team
- Audit MFA compliance monthly
- Review and approve exemption requests
- Enforce policy violations

## 5. Compliance and Enforcement

- **Non-compliance:** Access will be revoked until MFA is enabled
- **Violations:** May result in disciplinary action per HR policy

## 6. Policy Review

This policy will be reviewed annually or after significant security incidents.
